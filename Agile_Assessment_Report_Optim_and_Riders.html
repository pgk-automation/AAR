Agile Assessment Report: Optim and Riders
Introduction

This Agile Assessment was conducted as part of the Agile Transformation initiative to evaluate Agile process maturity, execution predictability, and flow efficiency, while considering inputs from Product & Delivery organization.

This report captures opinions, challenges, and insights gathered through self-assessments from the POD team and feedback from key Product and Delivery stakeholders.

Its objective is to surface key observations from Product, Delivery, and Team maturity, along with recommendations from the Agile Coach to address gaps through targeted Agile Transformation.

The report also provides summarized takeaways across key dimensions for a quick executive snapshot.

Based on the consolidated assessments, a pragmatic Stabilize, Adopt, and Optimize-to-Scale improvement roadmap has been defined, with a clear focus on improving delivery predictability, quality, transparency, and measurable business value realization.

Summary
Backlog & Planning Governance
  • Establish strong backlog governance by enforcing end-to-end traceability (roadmap ‚Üí epics ‚Üí stories), standardized DoR/DoD and INVEST criteria, and tooling controls that prevent progression without readiness compliance.
  • Strengthen PI and sprint readiness through disciplined, time-boxed backlog refinement, formal PI readiness checkpoints, and sprint readiness checks covering scope clarity, capacity, dependencies, and sprint-goal alignment.
  • Improve planning predictability by committing PI objectives only after backlog shaping, using capacity and velocity data, mandating confidence voting, and actively acting on confidence outcomes through refinement, re-scoping, or risk mitigation.
  • Reinforce Product Owner, BSA, Scrum Master, and pod team accountability for backlog quality, prioritization, readiness validation, sprint discipline, and controlled handling of unplanned or production work.
  • Elevate feedback and alignment via structured interim demos and mandatory system demos, with clear linkage to sprint goals, acceptance criteria, PI objectives, and business value, and with feedback tracked as actionable backlog items.
  • Institutionalize continuous improvement by reviewing backlog readiness, demo effectiveness, INVEST adherence, sprint discipline, and change management practices through retrospectives and Inspect & Adapt forums.
Delivery Predictability & Flow
  • Strengthen delivery predictability by standardizing sprint and PI-level burndown usage, ensuring real-time updates, mandating regular reviews, and coaching teams to interpret trends and take corrective action early.
  • Establish and continuously refine pragmatic Definition of Ready (DoR) and Definition of Done (DoD) standards, using delivery outcomes, defect trends, and regular retrospectives and Inspect & Adapt sessions to validate effectiveness.
  • Drive flow-based execution by enforcing WIP limits, monitoring cycle and lead time, tracking impediment aging, and publishing flow metrics to support data-driven delivery decisions.
  • Reinforce disciplined defect management through clear Product Owner accountability for prioritization, automated alerts and escalation for critical defects, and regular inspection of defect handling effectiveness.
  • Improve risk transparency and mitigation by standardizing ROAM practices, mandating early risk identification during planning events, maintaining visible risk dashboards, and reviewing risk communication effectiveness in ongoing forums.
Enablement & Continuous Improvement
  • Define and execute a clear GenAI use-case roadmap, prioritizing high-value areas such as backlog refinement, story and acceptance-criteria generation, requirement analysis, and root-cause analysis (RCA).
  • Embed GenAI accelerators into standard delivery workflows (refinement, documentation, sprint preparation, retrospectives) rather than treating them as optional or ad hoc tools.
  • Establish clear ownership and accountability by assigning GenAI use-case champions within pod teams to drive adoption, consistency, and continuous improvement.
  • Standardize a robust RCA framework with clear quality criteria, enforce action tracking through closure and outcomes, and integrate RCA reviews into retrospectives and Inspect & Adapt.
  • Measure and demonstrate GenAI value using productivity, quality, and outcome metrics; scale proven GenAI-enabled practices and coach teams with low adoption or weak results.
Enablement & Continuous Improvement
  • Standardize a robust RCA framework with clear quality criteria (problem statement, root cause, contributing factors, systemic gaps) and reject incomplete or superficial analyses.
  • Ensure RCA actions are tracked through closure and measurable outcomes, not just documented, to drive real improvement.
  • Embed RCA reviews into retrospectives and Inspect & Adapt forums to systematically apply learning across teams.
  • Scale proven GenAI-enabled RCA practices and actively coach teams with low adoption or poor-quality RCA outcomes.
Engineering Excellence
  • Track automation coverage and effectiveness as core quality metrics, with visibility into trends and outcomes.
  • Integrate automated functional testing into CI pipelines as a mandatory quality gate, prioritizing critical paths and high-risk functionality.
  • Allocate explicit sprint capacity for test automation, automation gaps, and test debt reduction to prevent quality erosion.
  • Reserve dedicated capacity per sprint and PI for technical debt reduction and treat it as a first-class delivery commitment.
  • Review automation and technical debt trends during Inspect & Adapt to ensure accountability, effectiveness, and sustained improvement.
Product Ownership & Business Alignment
  • Engage the LTIM team early in solution finalization, epic prioritization, and business value discussions to enable timely insights and proactive alignment.
  • Establish clear ownership for KPI definition, maintenance, and communication to ensure consistency and accountability.
  • Embed KPIs into PI Planning, execution, and retrospectives, making them integral to delivery and decision-making.
  • Provide shared, transparent KPI dashboards across Product, Delivery, and Pod teams, enabling leaders and teams to make empirical, data-driven decisions.
Team Ways of Working
  • Enforce strong execution discipline by time-boxing Scrum events with clear agendas, standardized carryover handling, and consistent facilitation to maintain focus and predictability.
  • Strengthen backlog and sprint hygiene by ensuring right-sized stories, clear acceptance criteria, aligned refactoring decisions, and controlled mid-sprint changes.
  • Clarify ownership and accountability for Jira hygiene, including timely updates, accurate status reporting, and transparent communication of progress and risks.
  • Build end-to-end domain knowledge through structured onboarding, walkthroughs, and shared documentation to reduce dependency bottlenecks and execution errors.
  • Use retrospectives and Inspect & Adapt to systematically address discipline gaps, spillovers, and recurring execution issues, focusing on root causes rather than symptoms.
  • Monitor capacity, workload, and spillover trends to identify recurring overload patterns and drive sustainable workload balancing.
